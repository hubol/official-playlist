The official Hubol music playlist

  • What I Am - Edie Brickell & New Bohemians

    My mother used to listen to this song a lot when I was young.

    I love the plucky guitar in the bridge.

  • American Life - Madonna

    My family used to listen to this album a lot in the car. I remember us all singing along while driving to visit my grandmother.

    I remember my mother really enjoying the fuck it and the goofy-ass rap verse in this song.

    The production on this album is pretty interesting.

  • countryp.mid - Unknown

    A charming piece of MIDI music heard in the desert town in Oddwarg Animal RPG.

    The music begins playing as soon as the text A great evil has entered the world. appears on screen.

    Oddwarg Animal RPG is the first Game Maker game that really captivated me. I was using my father's laptop while visiting my grandmother in Florida in 2006 when I discovered it on the now-defunct Game Maker Games website. My parents didn't purchase games very often for us, so this lengthy, free platform adventure provided a lot of entertainment for my little brother and I. Eventually, I decided I wanted to learn to make games and reached out to Oddwarg via e-mail, asking how he created the name entry field. I wondered if he had to program every possible letter combination, including the one for my unusual name.

    Needless to say, I can't help but feel nostalgic listening to the music.

  • Mutual Blue - the Oddwarg

    I stayed in touch with Oddwarg via e-mail for a couple years after our first contact. I would usually ask questions about how to execute a particular task in Game Maker and within a week or so he would respond with Game Maker source code that I could play with. Eventually, he stopped responding. I thought I had irritated him, but it turned out that he had to perform mandatory military service. During his absence, I sent him copies of my latest games including BEN!!, a game about a ball with a face, and CRAZ'D!, a game about exploring a large world as an insane asylum escapee. When he returned, he said he enjoyed CRAZ'D!, which I was flattered by, and that soon he would be releasing his game Madness. He offered to have me playtest it. Of course, I accepted.

    This is the title screen music from Madness. It is quite nice.

  • Bombastic Yellow Sheep - the Oddwarg

    The overworld area of Madness features 8 different music tracks that become available based on the player's progress and system time.

    This song plays during the daytime after the player has defeated a substantial number of wizards.

    The title and melody are quite playful.

  • Timelapse Kingdom - C418

    I really enjoyed the music in Minecraft and soon became interested in the rest of the composer's music.

    This album features very long songs, which was apparently a design goal. There is a lot of simple building and layering going on.

    Nowadays, I don't really listen to this music. But it was important for me at one point.

    I remember getting in trouble for defiantly listening to this in my headphones during the school news.

  • total drag - C418

    This was another favorite of mine. The instruments are very innocent and pleasant. I am transported to riding the school bus in winter.

    The album explores a few motifs, and I was inspired to incorporate motifs in the soundtracks for my game 2.

    The cover design inspired the color palette for my games JASON AND THE ARGONAUTS, 2, and 121.

    I also recorded a cover of this song which is heard at the end of JASON AND THE ARGONAUTS.

  • The Things We Saw - GUTS.WAV

    Music by my friend Matthew. I would love to perform with him sometime.

    I used to listen to this album while folding my family's clothes.

  • My Way Clusterfucked - Rasmus Hungnes

    Oddwarg's brother created this "remix" of My Way performed by Frank Sinatra. Me being me, I had never heard the original before this.

    The arrogance of the lyrics and vocal delivery combined with the "clusterfuck" effect is quite excellent.

  • Benny (the Bouncing Bump) - The Residents

    Oddwarg introduced me to The Residents around 2012. When I first heard this song, I was offended to hear general MIDI instruments outside of the context of a video game.

    But now I love it.

  • Just For You - The Residents

    This was the first song on the first CD I ever purchased. It is an amusing reinterpretation of The Residents' cover of We Are The World.

    This seemingly abridged recording omits a verse:

    I am a fake!
    I am a rip-off!
    I will definitely
    Shit on you to get off!

    I enjoy the singing character's arrogance and misery. The mispronunciation of the word "sycophant" appears in every recording.

    Of particular note is the backup singer's smooth, sine tone-like voice.

  • Banana Fanna - Charles Bobuck

    When The Residents revealed themselves as the electronic-metal trio "Randy, Chuck, and Bob" after nearly 40 years, it enabled Charles Bobuck, their composer, to release music under his own name.

    The title of the song comes from The Name Game:

    Chuck, Chuck, Bo-buck
    Banana, Fanna, Fo-fuck

    This reissued version of the album prefers tuned percussion over discordant thumps and crashes in a handful of tracks.

    This was the first song on the second CD I ever purchased. I listened to it several mornings while working the opening shift at the local Domino's Pizza where I was manager.

  • The Boat Song - Sun Stains
    I started choking
    You took to smoking

    Sometimes I listen to this while doing dishes.

  • Fat Boy - Sun Stains

    This is a song from 2013-ish by my old friend who I knew as "Tipp". Nowadays he records under the name "John King Cave".

    The mumbled vocals and bubblegum synths are very lovely.

    I would love to one day perform this song.
